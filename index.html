Itinerary

2 April 2026 — Arrival + first orientation

Travel day

Evening plan: Arrive in Rome, transfer to Hotel Santa Maria Maggiore, check in, and keep the night light. Walk to the Basilica di Santa Maria Maggiore, then continue into Monti for dinner and a relaxed first look at the city.

Suggested time blocks: 20:00–21:00 arrival and hotel check-in · 21:00–21:30 Santa Maria Maggiore exterior and square · 21:30–23:00 Monti walk and dinner.

Brief history: Santa Maria Maggiore is one of Rome’s four major papal basilicas and one of the earliest great churches dedicated to the Virgin Mary, showing the transformation of Rome from imperial capital to Christian center.

3 April 2026 — Ancient Rome + UNIQLO

Republic & Empire

08:00–09:00 Breakfast and walk toward the Colosseum area.

09:00–10:30 Colosseum. Focus on the amphitheater from inside or outside depending on tickets. Best views are from the Piazza del Colosseo and the upper approaches.

10:30–13:00 Roman Forum and Palatine Hill. Walk the Via Sacra, see the political and ceremonial core of Rome, then move up to the Palatine for views over the Forum and Circus Maximus.

13:00–14:00 Lunch near the Forum or Monti.

14:00–15:00 Capitoline Hill and Piazza del Campidoglio for one of the best overlook points into the Forum.

15:00–16:00 Piazza Venezia and the exterior of the Vittoriano to connect ancient and modern national Rome.

17:00–18:00 UNIQLO stop at Roma Termini on the way back toward the hotel. Use this window for clothing basics or anything you want before the Florence day.

18:30 onward Dinner around Monti.

Brief history: This is the backbone of Rome’s early story: the Forum was the civic heart of the republic, the Palatine was tied to the city’s legendary origins with Romulus, and the Colosseum represents imperial spectacle and state power under the Flavian emperors.

4 April 2026 — Vatican City + Decathlon

Papal Rome

07:30–08:30 Early start and transfer to Vatican City.

08:30–11:30 Vatican Museums. Move through the great collections toward the Raphael Rooms and the approach to the Sistine Chapel.

11:30–12:15 Sistine Chapel. Pause here for Michelangelo’s ceiling and Last Judgment.

12:30–14:00 Lunch near the Vatican walls or Borgo.

14:00–16:00 St. Peter’s Basilica. Watch the nave, Michelangelo’s Pietà, Bernini’s baldachin, and the immense scale of the church.

16:00–17:00 St. Peter’s Square and Via della Conciliazione for the urban view back toward the basilica.

17:30–20:00 Decathlon stop in Rome for rugby goods before returning to the hotel. Keep this as the dedicated sports-shopping slot.

Brief history: Vatican City grew from the site associated with the martyrdom and burial of Saint Peter. Over centuries the papacy built its spiritual and political center here, especially from the Renaissance onward, making it one of the most important religious and artistic complexes in the world.

5 April 2026 — Florence day trip

Renaissance day

06:30–07:30 Early breakfast and walk to Roma Termini.

07:30–09:00 High-speed train to Florence. Aim for Firenze Santa Maria Novella.

09:00–10:00 Walk from the station into the historic center.

10:00–11:30 Piazza del Duomo: Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore, Baptistery, and Giotto’s Campanile from the exterior or with timed entry if booked.

11:30–12:30 Piazza della Signoria and Palazzo Vecchio exterior. This is the political core of republican Florence.

12:30–13:30 Lunch in the center.

13:30–14:30 Ponte Vecchio and Arno river walk.

14:30–16:00 Santa Croce area or flexible museum slot if you prebook something open that day.

16:00–18:00 Slow coffee / gelato / final walk through the center.

18:00–20:00 High-speed train back to Rome.

20:00 onward Late dinner near the hotel.

Brief history: Florence rose from medieval commune to banking and artistic powerhouse, especially under the Medici. It became one of the birthplaces of the Renaissance, where classical ideas were revived and transformed into a new vision of art, architecture, and civic life.

6 April 2026 — Departure

Return flight

Morning plan: Check out and head to the airport for DY1871 departing Rome at 10:55.
